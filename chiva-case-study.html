Portfolio Case Study · Emily Gildner

Unsticking a Stalled
Global Education Program

How I brought structure, momentum, and educational integrity to an international medical training initiative that had been dormant for four years.

Program Global CHIVA
Domain Medical Education
Platform TalentLMS
Audience Global Healthcare Professionals

01 · Situation

A program in progress since 2021 — that hadn't progressed.

CHIVA (Conservative and Hemodynamic treatment of Venous insufficiency in Outpatients) is a minimally invasive technique for treating varicose veins using ultrasound. It's clinically effective but underrecognized globally — and the physician SMEs behind the program knew it needed a structured educational pathway to change that.

The problem: by the time I joined, the program had been in development since 2020–2021 and had stalled. There was no functional eLearning, no assessment framework, and no clear delivery pathway. Motivated SMEs, but no educational architecture to work from.

My role: educational consultant embedded with a team of international physician SMEs — bridging the gap between clinical expertise and sound instructional design.

02 · Challenges

High stakes. High complexity. Low bandwidth.

🌐

Cross-cultural SME collaboration — physician experts working across different languages, time zones, and clinical contexts.

⏱

Competing demands on SMEs — busy clinicians with limited time for curriculum development work.

🏗

No existing infrastructure — no modules, no assessment strategy, no LMS configuration.

📋

Scope that needed containing — a multi-tier program (Tiers 1–3) requiring phased planning to avoid scope creep.

03 · Approach

Structure first. Momentum second.

I came in as an educational consultant, not just a designer. That meant my first job was to build trust and shared language with SMEs who were experts in their clinical domain but new to structured learning design.

  • 1

    Established an instructional design framework for the program — translating clinical expertise into learner-centered objectives and tiered competency progression.

  • 2

    Provided ongoing assessment consultation — reviewing module content and providing feedback on assessment design, item quality, and alignment to learning objectives.

  • 3

    Configured and built within TalentLMS — designing the eLearning experience so it was accessible and appropriate for an international clinical audience.

  • 4

    Created a phased delivery roadmap — Modules 1–2 soft launched first; Modules 1–6 (Tier 1) on track for end of 2026, with Tiers 2 and 3 in pipeline.

  • 5

    Maintained forward momentum — by staying organized, proactive, and consistently translating "what SMEs know" into "what learners need."

They were impressed by my ability to lead so effortlessly and my knowledge of education and assessment — navigating a complex, international SME team with clarity and calm.

04 · Outcomes

From stalled to in-market — with a clear path forward.

2 Modules soft launched after years of inactivity
6+ Modules scoped and in progress for Tier 1 delivery
Global Reach — healthcare professionals across multiple countries

Beyond the deliverables: the program now has educational integrity it didn't have before. Assessment items are aligned to objectives. Content is structured for adult learners, not just clinical audiences. And the SME team has a partner who can translate their expertise into scalable education — instead of trying to build it alone.
